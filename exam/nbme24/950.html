misterdoctor69  The main thing that bothers me about this question is that if it is indeed nevus simplex, it's definitely a very non-typical presentation. The lesion described in this question is purplish (not pink) and it appears on the right side of the face (ie. neither back of neck nor midline). We can definitely rule out nevus flammeus because that is only seen in the setting of Sturge-Weber syndrome, which this patient has no signs of.
